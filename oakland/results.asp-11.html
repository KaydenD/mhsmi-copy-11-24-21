WEST BLOOMFIELD – Farmington Hills Harrison nearly forgot who it was.

The Hawks used their up-tempo style of play to establish a 13-point lead entering the fourth quarter of Wednesday Class A district semifinal with North Farmington, but in the final quarter Harrison tried to slow the game down and play it safe, which allowed the Raiders to storm back and force overtime at 46-46.

After falling behind four in the extra frame, the Hawks got them the lead, an up-tempo attack and eventually found a way to pick up a 56-52 victory.

“I liked the grit that we showed in overtime,” Harrison coach Stefon Wilson said. “You have to tip your hat to North Farmington, they didn’t quit. It was a good game and I am glad we got the win and are playing on Friday.”

North Farmington (15-6) was able to slowly chop away at the 13-point Harrison (14-6) lead in the fourth thanks to junior Kierra Crockett, who had 10 points in the period. The Raiders had some chances in the final minute to take the lead, but couldn’t convert. They still found themselves with the ball and in the closing seconds and junior Kelly Lusk connected with senior Alexa Cherney for a lay-in with one second to play, tying the score at 46-46.

“The kids just never gave up. To their credit they just kept battling,” North Farmington coach Jeff Simpson said. “It was a great comeback and I told them I was really proud of their effort to force overtime.”

Walker admitted that his team played it too safe in the fourth and that aided North Farmington in its comeback.

“We got passive and we’re not good at playing passive. We like to push the tempo and play with pace and for some reason we went into keep-away mode and that’s not our strength,” Wilson said. “It wasn’t by design for us, but I think the girls were becoming too aware of the clock and trying to slow things down.”

Crockett had the first two baskets of overtime to give North Farmington a 50-46 lead, but Harrison had its own rally, starting with a 3-pointer from senior Kristen Nelson. Junior Teddi Pickens added another 3-pointer moments later to give the Hawks the lead for good, as the Raiders were unable to produce much offense the rest of the session.

“We rode the momentum in the first minute of overtime, but then we just couldn’t get anything going and didn’t convert on some shots,” Simpson said. “(Harrison) is a good team and they made more plays in the overtime.”

Crockett finished with 24 points to lead all scorers while Sam Cherney had 14 points for the Raiders, who end the season with an OAA White title to their credit.

“The season was a success,” Simpson said. “Winning a division title, the first once since 2008, the girls accomplished a goal they set out to accomplish when the season started. The girls worked hard. Anytime you can win 15 games it is a successful season.”

The Hawks were led by Nelson, who had 21 points while Amber Stephens had 18 and Pickens finished with 11.

Harrison now advances to Friday’s district final where it will face Farmington Hills Mercy, who defeated West Bloomfield 48-37 in Wednesday’s other semifinal. The two will meet on Friday night at 7 p.m. from West Bloomfield High School.

“We just have to be ready for any scenario,” Wilson said of Friday’s game. “We are comfortable playing up-and-down, that is our strength. We like to make the game messy, so we hope Mercy comes with the pressure.”
