NEW SERVICE OPPORTUNITIES FOR STUDENTS
Check out these wonderful student volunteer events!

Caleb White Project Game Night at the Genesis House II Shelter Detroit
The Caleb White Project (CWP) is a Detroit-based non-profit with a two-fold mission: to help the homeless and less fortunate and to build youth leaders through volunteerism. Run by a youth advisory board consisting of board members under the age of 18, all CWP events and service projects are carefully designed with jobs for kids of all ages. 

Game Night at the Shelter has become the favorite project of almost everybody on the CWP team! After hearing that people living in the shelter must adhere to a strict curfew of 4:30pm, Caleb realized that this eliminated a huge part of social life for them. He recognized that his sports practices, extra-curricular activities and visits with friends all take place after 4:30pm. He wanted to do something to bring a little bit of fun back to their lives. Once a month, Caleb and his board members take pizza, pop, board games, and friends down to the shelter and spend their Saturday night hanging out with them.

For more information about this charity, visit calebwhiteproject.com.

Students are invited to volunteer and join in on Game Night at the Shelter on November 13. Mercy seniors Alyvia and Makenna Sanom are on the CWP board and will lead a game night where we will serve pizza to the moms and kids residing there, play games, and help with a craft activity. See the Sign-Up Genius here for all the details!

Questions for this service activity contact: Janet Cronin at janetmatt555@gmail.com.

CLASS OF 2023 COLLEGE PLANNING FOR JUNIOR PARENTS/GUARDIANS
This is a reminder of the 11/5 communication sent to Junior families. Mercy Counselors will provide services to all Juniors and their parents in an individual College Planning Meeting, which will take place in-person at Mercy HS. This is an opportunity for you and your Marlin to meet with their Counselor to focus on the following:
  • Review of transcript to discuss high school grade trends
  • Discussion about: college admission tests (ACT/SAT) and test preparation, building a college list, and identifying colleges the student has an interest in

  • Discuss student and Counselor roles and responsibilities during the college planning and admissions process

By Patricia Montemurri / Arcadia Publishing

Over 16,000 women are graduates of Mercy High School. Since 1945, when it opened as Our Lady of Mercy High School in northwest Detroit, its graduates have embodied the school motto: "Women Who Make a Difference."
