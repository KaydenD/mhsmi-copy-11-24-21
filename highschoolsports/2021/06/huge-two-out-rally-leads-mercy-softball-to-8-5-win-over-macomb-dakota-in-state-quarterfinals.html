Huge two-out rally leads Mercy softball to 8-5 win over Macomb Dakota in D1 state quarterfinals

Updated: Jun. 15, 2021, 11:04 p.m. | Published: Jun. 15, 2021, 11:04 p.m.
13

MHSAA softball quarterfinals: Farmington Hills Mercy 8, Macomb Dakota 5

By Jared Purcell | japurcell@mlive.com

DETROIT -- There is nothing like a two-out rally to turn the tide in a high school softball game.

On Tuesday night, the Farmington Hills Mercy softball pulled off its greatest two-out rally of the season and it could not have occurred at a better time.

Trailing Macomb Dakota, 4-2, in the bottom of the fifth inning with two outs, Mercy exploded for five runs before the inning came to an end to take a 7-4 advantage. It was a lead the Marlins would not surrender as they claimed an 8-5 victory and a clinched a spot in the Division 1 state semifinals.

“I think that is the most insane two-out rally I’ve ever seen from us this year,” said Mercy senior Maggie Murphy. “It was incredible and it was so great to be a part of. Just to see everyone put the work out there and for it to finally work, it was nice to see.”

In fact, the rally was so impressive that first-year Mercy head coach Corey Burras had a hard time comprehending it.

“That was impressive,” Burras said. “Anytime during the year to get five runs against competition like this? Wow. I want to look back at the scorebook and actually see how it happened. I’m not sure exactly how it happened.”

No matter how it happened, it happened. Now, the Marlins prepare to take on Allen Park at 5:30 on Thursday at Secchia Stadium on the campus of Michigan State University for a chance at a state championship game berth on Saturday.

“I knew it was going to be a battle,” Burras said. “I know how hard our batters work and how well they can adjust throughout the whole game. I knew we would come back. It was just a matter of when.”

Murphy sparked the rally by ripping a hit to left-center field, scoring senior Grace Nieto from third. However, the hit almost didn’t happen. It initially appeared as if the Dakota center fielder was going to pull off a great play and snag Murphy’s hit after a great effort to chase it down. However, the ball was able to escape the webbing of the outstretched mitt and, suddenly, Mercy had some momentum as it cut Dakota’s lead to 4-3.

Junior third baseman Kendall Spivey stepped up to the plate next and ripped a single up the middle to score Murphy and tie the game at 4-4. After an infield single by freshman pitcher Asia Barbato, sophomore Sabrina Shea put the ball in play in the infield and reached first base on a throwing error, allowing Spivey to score in the process.

Freshman catcher Kat Burras then capped the rally in a big way by scoring both Barbato and Shea with a single to left field.

“I had a lot of support from behind the dugout,” Kat Burras said. “Everyone was cheering me on, and I think that a key point with my hits, going 3-for-3, that was mainly my team pushing me along... It wasn’t for me, it was for the team and for the coaches and for the seniors. I didn’t want this to be their last game.”

Dakota attempted to respond in the top of the sixth when it put runners at the corners with no outs. However, Burras fielded a ball in play near the catcher, and instead of throwing the ball to first, she rifled a throw to third base where Spivey made the catch and an acrobatic move to catch the runner trying to return to the bag for the out.

I got the tag and I just barely got it,” Spivey said. “And then I looked around... It was a really big out to get at third. I think that really helped the game because it was in a tight predicament, but I feel like that moved the inning forward.”

After the next Dakota batter flew out to right field, Spivey got the last out of the inning by catching a low line drive at third base to end the inning and preserving the 7-4 lead. Of course, Spivey fielded the final hit of the game and threw the runner out at first to send the team into a celebration.

“Champions rise to the occasion,” Corey Burras said. “That just took the momentum. Shot to her and she tags her out at three. That takes away from the other team. She’s played real strong, especially toward the end of the season. She won the game for us in the regionals where she had a walk-off for us. She’s just risen to the occasion. Champions rise to the occasion and she absolutely is a champion.”

Dakota stole all of the momentum early when its sophomore starting pitcher Megan Nuechterlein launched a mammoth three-run home run over the left field wall in the top of the first inning.

After Mercy answered with an RBI single by Mercy in the bottom of the first, Dakota launched another home run in the top of the second as Megan Bryant’s frozen rope solo shot went over the center field wall to make it 4-1. An RBI double by Kat Burras in the bottom of the second cut Dakota’s lead to 4-2.

“It’s just so great to see that for (Burras),” Murphy said. “She’s worked so hard and being a freshman, it’s so good for her. I’m so happy for her.”

Nieto was able to score in the bottom of the sixth on a throwing error -- one of six errors made by Dakota in the game -- to make it 8-4. Dakota scored one run in the top of the seventh but could not get any closer.

After the two early home runs, Barbato settled in and did not give up a run the rest of the way to earn the win. Although she had just one strikeout in the win, Barbato retired 11 of 12 runners after giving up the leadoff home run in the second inning. She even had a nice highlight when she recorded three outs in three pitches in the top of the fifth. Sophia Paluk closed out the win on the mound in the top of the seventh giving up one run.
