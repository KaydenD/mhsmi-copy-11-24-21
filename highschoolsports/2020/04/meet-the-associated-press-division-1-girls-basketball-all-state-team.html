Meet the Associated Press Division 1 girls basketball all-state team

Updated: Apr. 02, 2020, 6:19 p.m. | Published: Apr. 02, 2020, 6:04 p.m.
1,714
shares
By Lee Thompson | lthomps2@mlive.com

The Associated Press announced its 2019-20 all-state girls basketball team for Division 1 in Michigan, recognizing the top high school players in the state.

East Lansing’s Aaliyah Nye was named AP Division 1 Player of the Year. She is one of five seniors on the all-state first-team and joins Southfield A&T’s Cheyenne McEvans as a repeat first-team selection.

The Associated Press all-state teams were selected by a panel of 11 Michigan sportswriters. The top vote-getters earned first-team honors while other players receiving more than one vote from the panel earned second-team honors. The rest of the nominees earned honorable mention.

FIRST-TEAM ALL-STATE

Jillian Brown, East Grand Rapids, Jr.

A big-time player with another prep season in front of her, she has verbally committed to Northwestern University. After earning honorable mention All-State a year ago, she elevated her game as a junior to pour in 22.4 points per game. She also tallied 8.0 rebounds, 4.2 assists and 3.0 steals while leading the Pioneers to a 23-1 record and a berth in the regional final.

Kailee Davis, Detroit Renaissance, Jr.

A big-game performer despite her 5-foot-4 frame, she was named Miss PSL after leading Renaissance to a 22-2 record and regional final berth. She hit seven 3-pointers in a 34-point effort in the PSL title game. The junior guard was good for 17.5 points, 4.0 assists and 5.5 steals per game. She has earned All-State recognition three times, earning honorable mention as a freshman and sophomore.

Cheyenne McEvans, Southfield A&T, Sr.

A key cog in last year’s run to a state runner-up finish, she came back even stronger for a sensational senior year. The 5-foot-10 guard put up big numbers every night, averaging 23.0 points and 10.0 rebounds. The repeat first-team All-Stater and University at Buffalo commit notched 3.7 assists, 3.2 steals and 2.0 blocks while leading the Warriors to the OAA Red title

Aaliyah McQueen, Flint Carman-Ainsworth, Sr.

A three-year fixture in a powerful Carman-Ainsworth lineup, she led her team to district and Saginaw Valley League South championships and a 19-5 record as a senior. The University of Illinois signee packed the stat sheet with 15.8 points, 8.1 rebounds, 3.6 assists and 3.4 steals per outing. She earned honorable mention All-State a year ago.

Alanna Micheaux, Wayne Memorial, Jr.

One of the most productive scorers in the state, she is a hot topic on the recruiting trail. The dominating, 6-foot-2 junior put up monstrous numbers with 26.4 points and 13.5 rebounds per game while leading her team to a 19-5 record and a district title. She has collected more than 40 college offers, including Big Ten interest.

Aaliyah Nye, East Lansing, Sr.

The Division 1 Player of the Year was runner-up in Miss Basketball voting after leading her team to a 23-1 record and a berth in the regional final. The two-time, first-team All-State performer has been a fixture on the statewide scene since pumping in 18 points in the state final as a sophomore. She delivered 16.1 points and 5.2 rebounds per game as a senior. She is signed with the University of Illinois.

Lauren Ross, Muskegon Reeths-Puffer, Sr.

The dazzling 5-foot-10 guard led her team to a 21-3 mark that is the best in school history, winning OK Black and district titles along the way. The four-year varsity starter averaged 26.0 points, 9.0 rebounds and 5.0 steals per game. The Ferris State University signee was honorable mention All-State a year ago.

Whitney Sollom, Hartland, Sr.

After earning honorable mention All-State the past three years, she closed out her career as a first-team selection. The 6-foot-5 defensive force helped Hartland to a program record for victories with a 23-1 mark and a march to the regional final. She produced 10.6 points and 9.7 rebounds per game and finished third in the Miss Basketball voting. She tallied 1,029 points and 824 rebounds in her varsity career.

Ally VanTimmeren, Jenison, Jr.

An impact player all three seasons of her prep career, she is a hot Division I college prospect with another prep season in front of her. The 6-foot-3 junior earned second-team All-State as a freshman and honorable mention a year ago. She averaged a double-double with 20.8 points and 10.1 rebounds per game while blocking 4.0 shots.

Arieonna Ware, Battle Creek Central, Jr.

A whirlwind basketball talent, she is rewriting the record book at her school. She already set program standards with 1,338 points and 750 rebounds in her career and punctuated her junior year with a record-setting 51-point game. The 5-foot-11 forward was unstoppable at 23.0 points and 12.0 rebounds per game while leading Central to a 19-5 record with a district crown. A second-team All-Stater a year ago, she has verbally committed to the University of Toledo.

COACH OF THE YEAR

FIRST TEAM

Tony Warren, Battle Creek Central

In his second season at the helm of his alma mater’s girls basketball program, Warren has Battle Creek Central trending on a whole new arc. After going more than a decade without more than six wins in a season, his team won 12 games a year ago then set the program record for wins with a 19-5 mark this year. He capped the season by guiding Central to its first district crown since 1987.
