Michigan enrollee Jess Mruzik is Gatorade National Volleyball Player of the Year

Updated: Jan. 23, 2020, 11:46 a.m. | Published: Jan. 22, 2020, 4:03 p.m.

By Jared Purcell | japurcell@mlive.com

FARMINGTON HILLS, Mich. -- She might not be in high school anymore, but Jess Mruzik is still hauling in awards for her efforts with the Farmington Hills Mercy volleyball team.

The award she received on Wednesday might be the best yet too.

In a surprise ceremony, Mruzik made the drive from Ann Arbor to Mercy High School to be presented with the Gatorade National Volleyball Player of the Year award, becoming the first volleyball player from Michigan to win the honor.

“This is such an amazing award and so many people have come before me who have turned out to be great volleyball players and great people,” Mruzik said. “It’s just unreal.”

This last fall, Mruzik was Mercy’s star player as she helped guide the volleyball team to its first state championship. In her stellar career at Mercy, she won the Gatorade Michigan Volleyball Player of the Year award as a junior and senior while also snagging the state’s Miss Volleyball award in November. VolleyballMag.com also dubbed Mruzik it’s choice for player of the year as well.

“I don’t really aim for all of these accolades that I’ve won,” Mruzik said. “It’s definitely super amazing to have won all of these awards throughout this year and over my volleyball career. But this isn’t why I play the game. I play the game because I love the sport and I love my teammates and the competitive atmosphere that comes along with playing volleyball. But this is amazing right now.”

Mruzik, a 6-feet-2 outside hitter, won the award by a landslide vote.

Because Mruzik enrolled early at the University of Michigan on Jan. 9, her family had to think of a clever way to drive back to Mercy to accept the award. Mruzik said she thought she was returning to Mercy for interviews with her high school teammates for a story on how special the championship season was. Everyone kept the award a secret from Mruzik until she stepped into Mercy’s small theater room where family, friends and Mercy teammates and coaches were waiting to surprise her.

Struck with emotion the moment she opened the door, Mruzik was thoroughly suprised. She was handed the award as she walked into the room and it did not take long for her former teammates to swarm her.

“The first thing I saw were all my teammates, and that’s so special for me,” Mruzik said. “I couldn’t have done it without them. I’ve been with a lot of them for so many years of my life and throughout my volleyball career. Having them here, I wouldn’t have won this award without them.”

It’s the second time Mruzik has been surprised with an award as she was presented the state’s Miss Volleyball award during her November signing ceremony.

“Well, I definitely didn’t think my family could keep surprises as well as they’ve proven to,” Mruzik said. “It’s unreal. All of the awards that I’ve gotten over the years, and even just this past year, they’re so special and they mean so much to me.”

Mruzik is a 6-foot-1 outside hitter and she led the Marlins to a 59-1 record en route to the state title. Along the way, Mruzik compiled 495 kills, 209 digs, 47 service aces and 19 blocks while posting a .635 kill percentage and a .540 hitting percentage.

“It’s just amazing for Jess and, actually, for us at Mercy,” Mercy volleyball coach Loretta Vogel said. “I don’t think Mercy’s ever had this level of accomplishment by an athlete. Clearly, for me in my career, I never thought I was going to be in this kind of a situation. It’s just wonderful for this school, the players, our program. Jess is just such a wonderful lady. It’s well deserved.”

Past winners of Gatorade National Player of the Year awards include fomer NFL quarterback Peyton Manning, former US Nation Team soccer player Abby Wambach, NBA player Karl-Anthony Towns, baseball Hall of Fame inductee Derek Jeter and many other sports icons.

Away from Mercy, Mruzik built a strong national and international résumé during her high school career. In 2019, she was captain of the USA Volleyball Youth National Team, leading the USA to capture a gold medal at the FIVB Girls U18 World Championships in Egypt. She even earned tournament MVP honors in the process.

With all of her high school goals accomplished, Mruzik turns her attention to a hopefully fruitful career at Michigan and beyond.

“I’m looking to win a national championship with Michigan in my time there,” Mruzik said. “I definitely think that we’re capable of doing that throughout the four years that I’m there. I don’t know, we’ll just see where volleyball takes me. Maybe Olympics, maybe pro. We don’t know yet.”

Mercy also took the opportunity to retire Mruzik’s No. 33 uniform, presenting her with her framed jersey.

“This is definitely a way to go out with a bang,” Mruzik said. “I came into this program wanting to build it up and Mercy volleyball already had such a great reputation with the volleyball program, the history of the school and everything. But I just wanted to take it to the next level and I think that’s what my teammates and I really did this year.”
