Gladwin’s Dayna Fennell blasts MHSAA record 19 home runs in final season

Updated: Aug. 21, 2019, 9:30 p.m. | Published: Jun. 10, 2016, 3:15 a.m.
By Ryan Yuenger | ryuenger@mlive.com

Nine games into her senior season, Gladwin senior Dayna Fennell was averaging a home run per game.

But it wasn't until Fennell hit her 15th home run – her third in a game against Evart after hitting two grand slams – that Flying G's coach Jill Keefer thought she could reach the MHSAA single-season record of 19.

“I know that seems kind of late,” Keefer said. “But that’s when I thought ‘she could really do this.”

In a way, Keefer was right in her uncertainty, as Fennell sat at 16 home runs heading into the Flying G's last tournament of the season at Ogemaw Heights.

That day, Fennell made history, blasting three home runs – one in each game – to tie Parchment's Kassidy Butler for the state record, set last year. 

She also put Gladwin softball on the statewide map.

"Everybody started asking about this girl," said Keefer. "They all started coming down to see her hit a home run. The 9 and 10 News even came down and filmed her."

Keefer said she also hopes The Delta College commit will bring more players into the Gladwin softball program.

“I’ve already asked the team ‘who’s going to hit 19 next year?’” Keefer joked. “But there’s definitely going to be more interest in Gladwin softball."

Gradual Progression

As a little-leaguer, Fennell was always amazed that players could hit one home run, let alone 19.

"I didn't honestly think I'd ever hit one either," said Fennell, to the 9 & 10 News. "In little league… I was wondering how people could hit home runs and I just pushed myself hard during my freshman year and then so on and so forth."

Her progression was gradual, but she came out of the gate swinging as a freshman and tied the Gladwin record with three home runs.

As a sophomore, she re-broke the record with four round-trippers, and broke it again with six as a junior.

Seven games into the 2016 season, she had already broken the record again.

“I’ve had the opportunity to watch her grow the last four years,” Keefer said. “She’s always had a power arm and bat, but she has continued to work hard and hit in the offseason more than ever before.”

Fennell ended her career tied for 10th in the state with 32 home runs.

In addition to the home runs, Fennell finished her senior season with a .465 batting average, 11 doubles, 46 runs scored and a school record 65 RBIs.

She also led Gladwin to a school-best 20-10 record while earning All-Conference and All-district honors.

“Dayna is such a strong-willed kid with a big heart,” Keefer said. “She’s unwilling to give up and doesn’t let anyone else give up – that’s what has led her to be where she is right now.

“It’s ironic that her power is also in her personality.”
