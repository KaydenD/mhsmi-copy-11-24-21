FOCUS: HOPE ANNUAL FOOD DRIVE
Friday, November 19 - Tuesday, November 23
Mercy values in action! As we enter into this season of thanks, one of the ways that Mercy High School expresses gratitude is by giving back to the community. For decades we have been an important partner to Focus: HOPE in their efforts to serve those in the Metro Detroit area. Focus: HOPE provides assistance to over 40,000 low income seniors every year through monthly food assistance, health screenings and so much more. 

All students and staff will sign up for select food items to donate along with a $5 contribution toward perishable items. Food donations and $5 collection will be during Adviser Group Friday, November 19, Monday, November 22 and Tuesday, November 23. To meet our school goal of filling half a semi-truck, we highly encourage students to bring in above and beyond what they have signed up for, yet we understand that this may not be possible for all Mercy families. 

Mercy High school is proud to be one of Focus: HOPE's largest and most influential donors. We thank you in advance for helping us make this a successful food drive!

NEW MERCY HIGH SCHOOL MICHIGAN BOOK
Images of America Mercy High School Michigan Book
By Patricia Montemurri / Arcadia Publishing

Over 16,000 women are graduates of Mercy High School. Since 1945, when it opened as Our Lady of Mercy High School in northwest Detroit, its graduates have embodied the school motto: "Women Who Make a Difference." This book commemorates Mercy High's 75th anniversary and reflects the impact of "Mercy Girls" on their communities, country, and around the world.
