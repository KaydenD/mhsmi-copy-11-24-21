Mercy rallies to defeat Dakota in quarterfinal softball game

Share this:

  • Click to share on Twitter (Opens in new window)
  • Click to share on Facebook (Opens in new window)
  • Click to print (Opens in new window)
By George Pohly | gpohly@medianewsgroup.com and Chuck Klonke | Macomb Daily
PUBLISHED: June 15, 2021 at 10:51 p.m. | UPDATED: June 22, 2021 at 10:00 a.m.

A three-run homer by Dakota’s Megan Nuechterlein in the first inning and a solo shot by Megan Bryant in the second sent Farmington Hills Mercy into some serious discussions in the Marlins’ dugout.

“I think after those four runs we figured out what we had to do different, what we needed to improve on, what pitches we should throw and what pitches we shouldn’t throw,” said freshman catcher Kat Burras after Mercy came back to defeat the Cougars 8-5 in a Division 1 quarterfinal game Tuesday at University of Detroit Mercy.

“We evaluated batters, where to pitch them, where not to pitch them. We noticed they were hitting the inside pitches really well, the high pitches, curveballs and rise balls. We decided to go low and outside and make sure they couldn’t get those. Kind of work off the plate and maybe the ump would give us some of those calls.”

The adjustments worked because freshman pitcher Asia Barbato didn’t allow another run through the sixth inning. Dakota scored its final run in the seventh off another freshman pitcher, Sophia Paluk, when Natalie Lerchenfeld doubled and score on Angela Petrovitch’s groundout.

Mercy coach Corey Burras had confidence in his team, but admitted Dakota’s early power show gave him some uneasy moments.

“That was scary,” he said. “They came out swinging, their crowd was into it, all the adrenalin they had, I knew it was going to be a battle. But I know how our team battles and how they adjust during the game. I knew we were going to come back. It was just a matter of when. If we can get two looks at any pitcher I know we’re going to move on the ball.”

Mercy’s time came in the fifth inning. Grace Nieto started the rally with a single, moved to second on a sacrifice and scored on Murphy’s double. Kendall Spivey and Barbato followed with RBI singles and Burras capped the uprising with a two-run single.

Maggie Murphy, who came into the game with 66 RBI, drove in the Marlins’ first run with a single in the first inning. Kat Burras drove in a second-inning run with a double, the first of her three hits. Mercy got its final run in the sixth on a wild pickoff throw.

“Five runs against competition like this. Wow. I’m not sure how it happened,” Corey Burras said. “We were able to make the adjustments. It’s a credit to the amount of work they put in. We’re always challenging ourselves. We’re comfortable being uncomfortable. We trust our process.”

Dakota threatened in the sixth when Briana Bumbalough walked and Nuechterlein singled. Grace Roeske followed with a hard line drive that third baseman Kendall Spivey snagged and stepped on third for a double play. Nicole Syracuse singled but Spivey grabbed another line shot by Bryant to end the inning.

“That’s Kendall. Champions rise to the occasion,” Corey Burras said. “That took away their momentum right there.”

It wasn’t a typical game in the field for Dakota, which made six errors behind Nuechterlein.

“Today was just not our day,” said Cougars coach Dan Vitale. “We made six errors that really hurt us. That’s not typical of our team, but that’s the way it rolls sometimes. Mercy is a great team. I hope they continue to do well.

“Our kids never gave up all year long, they were battling in the seventh. We have nine seniors that have been great leaders, but our future is looking really good. We have some young talent coming up. I’m excited for next year.”
