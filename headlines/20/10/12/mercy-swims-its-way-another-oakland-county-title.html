Mercy swims its way to another Oakland County title

Published on 10/12/2020

By: Drew Ellis dellis@medianewsgroup.com

Oakland Press Article

Photo Caption: The Farmington Hills Mercy swim team captured the 2020 Oakland County championship on Saturday. The Marlins swam at one of eight satellite events this year in order for the meet to be conducted. Mercy competed at Groves High School. (KEN SWART - MediaNews Group)

Farmington Hills Mercy won itself another Oakland County title in girls swimming and diving on Saturday, but did it in a different style.

The Marlins narrowly defeated Cranbrook-Kingswood for the title, 285-266, in an event that was scattered across Oakland County.

Eight different sites hosted four teams and times were tabulated together in order to determine event and team winners.

The new format was done this year to limit the number of athletes at a single site in order to help prevent the spread of COVID-19.

Click here for photo gallery.

Relays were a strong part of Mercy’s success on Saturday, as the Marlins took home the county title in two of the three relay events.

The team of Sydney Derkevorkian, Amylia Higgins, Katie Hermann and Greta Gidley won the 400-yard freestyle relay with a time of 3:35.65. Gidley joined Madeline Basa, Emma May and Samantha Diaz to win the 200 medley relay with a time of 1:47.71.

Gidley also went on to win the county title in the 50 freestyle with a time of 23.95. Diver Clara McCliment won the title for the Marlins as well with a score of 382.70.

Cranbrook’s team of Charlotte Trunsky, Hale Oal, Gwen Woodbury and Charlotte DeSantos won the 200 freestyle relay in a time of 1:37.96.

Woodbury went on to win two individual titles for the Cranes as well. She won the 200 freestyle in a time of 1:50.57, and won the 100 freestyle in 51.29.

Cranbrook’s Justine Murdock also took home two event titles. She won the 200 individual medley in 2:08.19, and won the 100 backstroke in 56.76.

Farmington’s Madeline Greaves won the county title in the 100 butterfly with a time of 56.33. She also went on to win the 100 breaststroke with a time of 1:04.74.

Novi’s Jimin Son won the 500 freestyle with a time of 5:06.42.
